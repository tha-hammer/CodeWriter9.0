The error is `~` applied to a non-boolean — `HasPackageJson` and `PkgJsonValid` were declared as untyped CONSTANTS so TLC treats them as model values, not booleans. The fix is to promote them (along with `CodebaseType`) to nondeterministically-initialised `\in`-variables so TLC knows their types and explores all combinations.

    MaxSteps

           "Deduplicate","FallbackCheck","Finish"}

    {s \in Skeletons :
        s.is_public /\ s.is_top_level /\ s.file \notin ExcludedFiles}

    {r \in RouteScans : r.file \notin ExcludedFiles}

    {c \in CliScans : c.file \notin ExcludedFiles}

RouteEPs ==
    {[file   |-> r.file,
      fname  |-> r.handler,
      etype  |-> "HTTP_ROUTE",
      route  |-> r.route,
      method |-> r.method] : r \in NonExcludedRouteScans}

    {[file   |-> c.file,
      fname  |-> c.cmd,
      etype  |-> "CLI_COMMAND",
      route  |-> NoVal,
      method |-> NoVal] : c \in NonExcludedCliScans}

    {[file   |-> b.file,
      fname  |-> b.fname,
      etype  |-> "CLI_COMMAND",
      route  |-> NoVal,
      method |-> NoVal] : b \in BinTargets}

SkeletonEPs ==
    {[file   |-> s.file,
      fname  |-> s.fname,
      etype  |-> "PUBLIC_API",
      route  |-> NoVal,
      method |-> NoVal] : s \in PublicTopLevelSkeletons}
